Quickstart Guide

Part 1

Create a new project on the homepage by clicking "Minecraft Skin" and turn off "Pose"

Part 2

Once in, go to File>Convert Project and select Generic Model.

Part 3

At this point, you'll have a Generic Model with pre-placed faces, you can begin texturing from here if you would like. Or you can edit each part on the side to change the shape of the model.
Let's start by just making this model smaller, shrink the Y value of the body, arms, and legs to 8 and reposition them so they look like the image below.


Part 4

Now you'll need to generate a new set of UVs for this model on the left. Start by selecting all parts with CTRL+A and clicking "Create New Texture" in the texture Pane. Delete the old texture named "steve" and right click your currently selected cubes and go to texture>texture to assign them the newly created sheet.


Part 5

This part may need some artistic talent, but either way, do your best! Go to the paint tab in the top right pane above "Element" and you can now paint directly onto the model or UV Sheet.


Part 6

That's all there is to it! Feel free to add more cubes as you please in the outliner to make it more or less complex! Check out the examples page to see what's possible!
